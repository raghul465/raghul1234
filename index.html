Diagnostics


The "Diagnostics" section in our hospital management system serves as a comprehensive repository for all diagnostic tests, procedures, and findings conducted for individual patients. Through meticulous documentation and organization, we ensure that healthcare providers have access to essential information vital for delivering optimal patient care. From initial assessments to ongoing monitoring, our diagnostics module plays a crucial role in facilitating accurate diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient management


Blood Test


A blood test is used to measure various components of blood, such as red blood cells, white blood cells, and platelets.

X-ray


An X-ray is a type of imaging test that uses small doses of radiation to produce pictures of the inside of the body.

Ultrasound


An ultrasound is a diagnostic imaging technique that uses high-frequency sound waves to create images of organs and structures inside the body.

Electrocardiogram (ECG)


An ECG is a test that measures the electrical activity of the heart and is used to diagnose heart conditions.

MRI Scan


An MRI scan is a type of imaging test that uses powerful magnets and radio waves to produce detailed images of the body's internal structures.

CT Scan


A CT scan, or computed tomography scan, combines multiple X-ray images taken from different angles to create cross-sectional images of the body.

Colonoscopy


A colonoscopy is a procedure used to examine the inside of the colon and rectum for abnormalities or signs of disease, such as polyps or cancer.

Bone Density Test


A bone density test measures the strength and density of bones and is used to diagnose osteoporosis and assess fracture risk.
